Logarithmic Equalities

• Is an equation that involves the logarithm of an expression containing a variable.
• Logarithmic Equations are useful when solving equations/problems regarding exponential growth or decay.
• Logarithmic Equations are widely used in many fields involving mathematics such as: Engineering, Physics, and Computer Science.

THE STEP-BY-STEP IN SOLVING LOGARITHMIC EQUATIONS:

Step 1: Isolate the Logarithmic Expression.
If there are additional terms in the equation, isolate the logarithmic expression on one side.

Step 2: Apply the One-to-One property.
Use the fact that if log base b of x equals log base b of y, then x equals y. This property allows you to set the arguments of the logarithms equal to each other.

Step 3: Solve for the variable.
To solve for the variable, set the arguments equal and then solve the resulting equation.

Step 4: for Extraneous solutions.
It's possible for logarithmic equations to have extraneous solutions, so it's important to check the solutions in the original equation to ensure they are valid.

Step 5: State the Final Solution.
Present the solution or solutions to the logarithmic equation.
