Logarithmic Laws and Properties

-Logarithmic laws and properties are mathematical rules that govern the manipulation and simplification of logarithmic expressions.
Logarithms are mathematical functions that represent the exponent to which a fixed number (called the base) must be raised to obtain a given number.
The logarithmic laws and properties make it easier to perform calculations involving logarithms. Here are some fundamental logarithmic laws:

Properties of Logarithmic

These logarithmic laws are useful in simplifying logarithmic expressions, solving equations involving logarithms, and converting between logarithmic bases. They are fundamental tools in various areas of mathematics, physics, engineering, and other scientific disciplines.

Definition:

If by=x, then logb x=y. In other words, the logarithm of x to the base b is equal to y

Product Rule:

logb (yx )=logb x−logb y The logarithm of the product of two numbers is the sum of the logarithms of the individual numbers

Quotient Rule:

logb (yx)=logb x−logb y. The logarithm of the quotient of two numbers is the difference of the logarithms of the individual numbers.

Power Rule:

logb (xn)=n⋅logb x. The logarithm of a number raised to a power is the exponent times the logarithm of the base.

Change of Base Formula:

logb1 x=logb2 b1 logb2 x This formula allows you to change the base of a logarithm.

Zero Rule:

logb 1=0. The logarithm of 1 to any base is always 0.

Identity Rule:

logb b=1. The logarithm of a number to the same base is always 1.

Undefined Logarithms:

logb x is undefined for x≤0. Logarithms of non-positive numbers are undefined.

Logarithmic and Exponential Functions are Inverses:

logb bx=x and blogb x=x. Logarithmic functions and exponential functions are inverses of each other.

Laws of Logarithmic

1. Product Law:

logb (xy)=logb (x)+logb (y) This law states that the logarithm of the product of two numbers is equal to the sum of the logarithms of the individual numbers.

2. Quotient Law:

logb (yx )=logb (x)−logb (y) The logarithm of the quotient of two numbers is equal to the difference of the logarithms of the individual numbers.

3. Power Law:

logb (xn)=n⋅logb (x) This law states that the logarithm of a number raised to a power is equal to the product of the exponent and the logarithm of the base.
